Why should I donate blood?

Blood transfusion is needed for:

Women with complications of pregnancy, such as ectopic pregnancies and haemorrhage before, during or after childbirth
children with severe anaemia often resulting from malaria or malnutrition
People with severe trauma following man-made and natural disasters
Many complex medical and surgical procedures and cancer patients.

Benefits of Blood Donation

Donating blood gives individuals a sense of purpose and fulfillment as they know they are contributing to saving lives.
Regular blood donation is associated with a reduction in the risk of cardiovascular diseases.
It gives donors a medical check at no cost.
Boosts the production of red blood cells.
helps in maintaining iron levels in the body.

Who can Donate blood?

An individual who is in the age group of 18 to 65 years,can typically donate whole blood every 8 weeks (56 days).
An individual who weighs more than 45 kgs
An individual with normal blood pressure and haemoglobin levels.
An individual who does not have any infections like HIV,Hepatitis B or C ,Tuberculosis etc.
An individual who is not on certain medications.
